### Blog 1: Is your payroll compliant? A UK SME guide
**Title:** The 2024 UK SME Payroll Compliance Checklist
**Summary:** Staying on the right side of HMRC is the #1 priority for any small business. Between RTI filings, tax code changes, and Minimum Wage updates, it’s easy to miss a step.
**Key Points:** 
*   Real Time Information (RTI) – Submitting every pay run.
*   National Insurance (NI) – Keeping up with recent rate changes.
*   National Living Wage – Ensure all staff are in the correct age brackets.
*   Statutory Payments – Sick pay (SSP), Maternity (SMP), and Paternity pay.

### Blog 3: Auto-enrolment pensions explained for small business owners
**Title:** Auto-Enrolment Pensions: Your Ongoing Duties Made Simple
**Summary:** Setting up a pension is only the start. You have legal duties at every single pay run.
**Key Points:**
*   Assessment: Checking staff age and earnings every month.
*   Contributions: Calculating and paying the 3% employer and 5% employee shares.
*   Communications: Legally required letters to staff.
*   Re-declaration: Filing with the Pensions Regulator every 3 years.

### Blog 4: PAYE vs IR35 — what employers need to know
**Title:** PAYE, IR35, and Off-Payroll Working: A Guide for UK Employers
**Summary:** Hiring a "freelancer" isn't always as simple as it looks. HMRC's IR35 rules mean you might be responsible for their tax.
**Key Points:**
*   The Difference: Direct employees (PAYE) vs Contractors (IR35).

*   The Risk: Getting it wrong means YOU owe the back taxes, not the contractor.
