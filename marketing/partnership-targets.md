# Do My Payroll - Partnership Targets

## 1. Professional Service Partners
*   **Accountants (Small-to-Mid size):** Firms that don't have a dedicated payroll department.
*   **Bookkeepers:** Solo practitioners who handle day-to-day accounts but want to outsource the technical payroll/pension filings.
*   **HR Consultants:** They often advise on hiring but need a partner to handle the actual pay processing.
*   **Business Coaches:** They help SMEs scale and often identify payroll as a bottleneck for the owner.

## 2. Business Communities
*   **Local Chambers of Commerce:** Join and offer to write a guest blog on "2024 Payroll Legislation".
*   **BNI Groups:** Local networking groups with a "one person per profession" rule.
*   **Co-working Spaces:** (e.g., WeWork, Regus, or local independent hubs). Offer a free "Lunch & Learn" session.
*   **Industry Associations:** (e.g., British Hospitality Association, Federation of Small Businesses).

## 3. Tech Integrations (Opportunity for Content)
*   **Accounting Software:** Xero, QuickBooks, FreeAgent (Write "How to integrate your payroll with [Software]").
*   **Time & Attendance Apps:** Deputy, Planday (Target sectors like hospitality where rota integration is key).

## 4. How to Approach
1.  **Identify:** Find the decision-maker on LinkedIn (e.g., "Practice Manager" or "Founder").
2.  **Add Value:** Don't just ask for referrals. Offer a free resource or a guest post for their audience.
3.  **The "Reverse Referral":** Mention that you are looking for an [Accountant/HR Consultant] to refer your own clients to—this is the best way to start a reciprocal relationship.
