# Do My Payroll - Local SEO & Google Business Profile Guide

## 1. Why Local SEO?
Most small business owners look for local providers for services like payroll and accounting. Ranking in the "Map Pack" for your city is the most effective way to get free inbound leads.

## 2. Google Business Profile (GBP) Optimisation

### Step 1: Claim and Verify
*   Ensure the business is claimed at google.com/business.
*   Verify via phone or postcard.

### Step 2: Accurate Business Information
*   **Name:** "Do My Payroll" (Avoid keyword stuffing the name, but you can add a descriptor like "Do My Payroll - SME Payroll Services").
*   **Category:** Use "Payroll Service" (Primary), "Accounting Firm", "Business Consultant".
*   **Address:** Use a physical office address if possible.
*   **Phone:** Use a local area code number if possible.

### Step 3: Service Descriptions
*   List every service: PAYE Management, RTI Submissions, Auto-enrolment Pensions, CIS (Construction Industry Scheme) Payroll.
*   Use keywords naturally in descriptions.

### Step 4: Photos and Updates
*   Upload photos of the office, the team, and helpful graphics (e.g., "April 2024 Tax Update").
*   Post "Google Updates" at least once a week (similar to a social media post).

### Step 5: Reviews
*   Actively ask every happy client for a 5-star review.
*   **Pro Tip:** Reply to EVERY review (even the good ones) using keywords like "best payroll service in [City]".

## 3. On-Page Local SEO
*   Include your city/region in the `<title>` tag of your homepage.
*   Add a "Service Areas" section or page listing the towns/cities you serve.
*   Embed a Google Map on the contact page.

## 4. Citation Building
List the business on:
*   Yell.com
*   Thomson Local
*   Scoot
*   Cylex
*   Bing Places for Business
*   Apple Business Connect

**Crucial:** Ensure the Name, Address, and Phone (NAP) are identical across all sites.
